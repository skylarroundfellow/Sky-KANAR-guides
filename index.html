Armor Locations

A suit of armor is divided into 7 locations: 2 legs, 2 arms, front torso, rear torso, and head. Armor points are given based on the number of locations armored.

If a player has both upper arms (but not lower arms), or both lower arms (but not upper arms), they gain AP as if they had ONE arm location covered. If a player has both upper legs (but not lower legs), or both lower legs (but not upper legs), they gain AP as if they had ONE leg location covered.

Example: Samson has a plate breastplate (20), 2 plate legs (20x2), and 2 leather arms (5x2), for 70 total AP.

Armor Types

There are four types of armor in Kanar: Leather, Composite, Chainmail/Metal Scale and Plate.

Leather: This armor is made from the hides of animals. The leather is usually hardened to fit the wearer.
Composite: These are more advanced forms of leather armor. Some examples of this are studded leather, leather scale, and ring mail.
Chain/Metal Scale: Chainmail is composed of small metal rings interwoven to form a flexible material, which is shaped into hoods, vests, etc. Metal scale is similar to leather scale except that the scales are made of metal.
Plate: Plate armor is made of large metallic plates that are shaped and articulated to offer nearly complete protection of the entire body.

Spell Restrictions

An item may only have one Augment spell active on it at any one time A character may only have one Boon and one Protection spell of a single element active upon them at any one time For instance, they may have one Air Protection and one Earth Protection, or one Air Protection and one Air Boon, but never two Air Protections

If an item or character is successfully targeted by a spell of a higher level than one currently active upon them, and the existing effect conflicts with the new one as described above, the old spell ends and the new one takes effect For instance, if a character is currently under the effects of a Level 2 Water Boon, and they are successfully targeted by a Level 3 Water Boon, the Level 2 spell ends and the Level 3 spell takes effect

Please note: Some Mass spells state that all targets in the area gain the effects of a lower-level spell For the purposes of deciding which spell ends and which one takes effect, the Mass spell counts as the level of the spell listed in the description For example, someone under the effects of Courage of Aeracon may be within the area of a Mass Greater Courage spell – that spell would not replace the Courage of Aeracon, since it is a higher level than Greater Courage

Out-of-Game Mechanics for Item Creation

Off field: All Item creation occurring between events is done on-line and requires the use of a player bank. Go to www.kanar.club and fill out the appropriate item creation form. Please see the crafting packets for details on what you can craft in between events. Once your creation time has been completed, the game staff will give you a tag for your item at the following event.

On field: Once per event, a character may use a Craft, Herbalism, or Item Creation skill to craft or refine an item on field. To do so, they must spend two hours roleplaying the process. This roleplay must involve Phys. Reps. for appropriate tools and materials, simply miming the actions with no props is insufficient.

One prop (such as an anvil, a sewing basket, or a small workbench) should be marked with yellow tape and placed on a flat, stationary surface (such as the ground or a table) at the beginning of this time. If the marked item is moved, if the character falls unconscious, or if they move more than 10’ from the item at any point, this time is lost and they must start over. If the char- acter is successful, they may see an appropriate staff member for a tag for one of the following:

  1. One Non-Master Crafted Item which the char- acter could produce using their existing Craft skill(s), Herbalism skill, or Item Creation skills in one month or less. The character must still pay all coin and material costs for creating the item. If creating a potion or scroll, the crafter must successfully cast the spell being placed into the item during the roleplayed crafting time. This spell will be absorbed by the item upon casting
  2. One Master Crafted item which the character could produce using their existing Level 5 Craft skill(s) in one month or less. The player must first provide a tag for an identical Non-Master Crafted Item, as well as any difference in coin or material costs between a Master Crafted and Non-Master Crafted item of that type.

Armor Basics

In general, armor provides the player with extra AP (Armor Points). This AP is depleted first when taking damage. See the Combat section for a deeper dive into the mechanics.

Locations

In KANAR, armor is worn on specific locations on the body.
There are 7 in total:
   Head
   Rear Torso (Back)
   Front Torso (Chest)
   Left Arm
   Right Arm
   Left Leg
   Right Leg

The physical representation (physrep) needs to cover the majority of the location to count, but you can combine partial locations on the limbs. This means you could wear two bracers which cover both of your lower arms and count it as one location. Or wear two greaves and count that as one as well.

Each armor location can support only one type of armor at a time. You can't stack breastplate and a chain shirt for your Front Torso slot. You would need to decide which of the two filled the location.

A player must also have a tag for each location of armor for it to be considered in game.
You can wear armor without a tag but it will not provide any mechanical benefit.

If you have a metal helmet that covers the back of the head, it can protect you against a knock out suprise attack.

Types

There are 4 different types of armor available, each provides different levels of protection (Armor Points) per location:

TypeAC
Leather 5 AP
Composite 10 AP
Chain/Scale 15 AP
Plate 20 AP

Calculating AP

To calculate the AP of the armor you currently have equipped, you add up the current AP for each of the 7 locations.

In order for a location to count in the calculation it needs:
     * A physical representation
     * A tag
     * A current AP larger than 0.

Current AP starts off the same as the total AP for that type of armor.
When the armor takes damage, the current amount of AP it provides is reduced by the amount of damage taken.
If it is above 0 AP, it can be Repaired back up to the total AP for that type of armor.

In total, a player can benefit from a max of 140 of non-magical armor.
[Plate (20) x 7 locations]

The Total Max AP a player can get is 146, due to the armor rune enchantment.
(See Magical Armor)



Your Armor Move skill will determine how much of that AP you can utilize.
The AP you can utilize is limited to the lowest of the following:
     * Your Armor Move Skill ranks x 5
     * The current AP that you have equipped

One weird interaction to note is what happens when you don't have enough AP to fully utilize an armor location.
In particular, if you wear armor larger than your Armor Move, your armor will break when you run out of AP, even if the armor would typically have AP left over.

As an example:
If you are wearing a plate armor location [20AP], and only have 1 rank in Armor Move [5 AP]. That plate armor will break after it takes 5 damage.

Armor related Skills

In order to actually use any armor you need to have specialized skills.

Armor Move

This is a required skill to use any armor.
Each time you buy the skill, you gain the ability to use 5 more AP from any armor you wear up to the total AP of the armor itself.


This is a Phsyical Ability Skill, which means you can buy it multiple times.
It can be self taught, so after you learn from someone else once, you can teach it to yourself as many times as you want.
After that first rank, it only counts as one of your skills learned per event; regardless of how many ranks you pick up in that event's signout.

Each rank costs:

Cleric 15 XP
Rogues 15 XP
Mage 20 XP

This skill will allow you to create pieces of armor.

Like Armor move, you can purchase this skill multiple times at the same price.
Unlike Armor move, there are only 5 levels associated.

Costs per rank per Class:

Combat

Combat is the main purpose of Armor. You can think of armor as a temporary pool of Body Points that might be replenish-able, depending on what happens. We'll go more into that when we get to repair options

Taking damage

When you're hit by an attack, most of the time you'll take damage. This is the number that gets called out by the other creature/player. That number then gets subtracted from your health pools, in the following order:

MAP -> AP -> MAP* -> MBP -> BP

MAP = Magical Armor Points
AP = Armor Points
MAP* = Magical Armor Points for Wind Armor, Divine Protection
MBP = Magical Body Points
BP = Body Points

We'll go over the MAP and MAP* in the next section.

Any “No-Armor” attack ignores MAP and AP and goes directly to MBP then BP, bypassing armor completely
[Exceptions: Ice Mantle, Wind Armor, Divine Protection]

For armor in this guide, we're typically only concerned with the AP pool. I'll provide some info on the Magical Armor Points just for refence.
Within the AP pool, there's a priority for how damage is distributed toyour different locations.

The location with the smallest AP takes damage first till it is exhausted, then the next smallest and so on.

If all locations have the same AP and type, it's up to the player to decide which location takes damage first.

Walking through an example:

I have 1 location of leather armor and 1 location of chain armor with enough armor move to utilize all of it.
That would have a total of AP 20 (5 leather and 15 chain).

I take a hit for 3 damage.
All 3 damage goes into the leather location, leaving it at 2 AP. The chain location is at 15 AP.

I take another hit for 4 damage.
2 of that damage goes into the leather location, reducing it to 0. The last 2 damage goes into the chain armor, leaving it at 13.

When the leather armor went to 0, it became broken. This means it cannot be repaired without using a Mending Oil or via econ-based crafting.
(See Broken vs Destroyed)

Since the chain location wasn't broken or destroyed, that chain armor can now be repaired by an Armorsmith out of combat.

It takes between 1-5 minutes to repair one AP, depending on how skilled the Armorsmith is. (Level 1 translates to Novice, Level 5 translates to Master)

Continuing our example, a Master Armorsmith then repairs the armor by role playing out the fixing process for the next 2 minutes. (A Novice would spend 10 minutes)

Not explicitly in the rules, it's considered good form to remove the armor so you can more effectively role play fixing it. I've seen multiple armor smiths working on the same piece on field, to cooperatively minimize the time. I don't know where that sits officially.

Spell Restrictions

It's important to note that due to the way spell stacking works, you won't be able to have spells with the same element/type on you at the same time.


In regards to spells that provide you with MAP, you can only have one spell from each row active at a time:
Air Protection: Protection Series, Wind Armor
Earth Protection: Oak Hide Series, Divine Protection
Water Protection: Ice Mantle
Earth Augment: Bulwark Series

You can have a spell from each of the above rows active at a time.

If you do have multiple MAP available at the same time, the player decides which of the two spells takes the damage first.

Broken vs Destroyed

Broken

New for this rulebook, we have a status for Armor: Broken.

When your armor takes damage and is reduced to 0 AP. It is considered Broken.
(Previously, the armor was considered Destroyed.)

Destroyed

If your armor is hit by a Wither Armor spell, then it is destroyed.
The Player needs to rip up the tag into 2 pieces and turn into Econ. This is the same process as using a Potion/Scroll.

Wither Armor is particularly strong as there is only one way to actively counter it: Greater Spell Barrier. You'll have to cast it before Wither Armor is cast.
The Stainless spell/Alchemy only counters regular Wither, does not counter Wither Armor.

Your armor is safe from a Wither Armor if it has a Fortification Rune or Repair Rune

Repair options

Non-Broken (AP 1+ or Repair/Fortification Rune)

Non broken armor can have its AP restored on field
You have 2 main options for repair:

Option 1: The Craft - Armorsmithing skill.
It will take 1-5 minutes per AP depending on the Armorsmith's crafting level.
The Armor should be off of the player while role playing the repair actions

Option 2: Spells/Alchemy
If the Armor is Leather or Composite or other dead organic material
The Mend spell will repair 5 AP
If the Armor is Chain/Scale Mail or Plate
The Metal Mend Alchemy will repair 5 AP


Broken (AP 0)

Broken Armor can be repaired either On Field or Off Field.

On Field there are 2 options

Option 1: Mending Oil
This alchemy will return your broken armor to 1 AP, it can then be repaired by the options above.

Option 2: On field crafting.
This method will use the on field crafting rules to fully repair a location of armor.
It will return with full AP.

It's typically a bad idea to repair via this method, unless you can sell the item at full price, as you're missing out on crafting a large item w/out the time restriction.
An exception to this might be merged locations.


Off Field

Armor can be repaired using the same process as armor creation, the difference being that it costs half as much to repair broken armor, as it does to create new armor.
This reduction in cost is in both time and supplies, so a Plate would cost you 6 units and be created in 1 week.
(See Crafting between events)



Destroyed (Wither Armor)

There is no way to restore armor that has been Destroyed.

Please reach out to a GM, as they might be able to provide an alternative route.

Enchanting

These enchantments are vital at keeping your Armor from being broken or destroyed. This guide doesn't step through how to enchant.

Repair Rune

The Repair Rune allows the armor to auto repair at a rate of 1-3 AP a day.
Note, it specifically mentions “Armor”, so you cannot put this on any enchanted item.

Fortification Rune

The Fortification Rune cannot be broken or destroyed.
For the purpose of this guide, we're placing it on Armor, but this rune can be placed on any enchant-able item.

Physreps and you

The rule book does a pretty good job of providing information on what is acceptable.

This is a very subjective area, as you'll need to get staff approval for any armor you're wearing (for it to count as Armor. You may wear what you like for Aesthetic purposes)

The only area I have any experience in is Leather, and it's suggested to use 5-6 oz leather at a bare minimum.

It's highly recommended to use 2 layers when constructing your armor, or use 10-12 oz leather.


Due to the tag rules, you'll need to make sure every armor tag you have on your person has a physrep. This includes broken armor you have from another player, or armor you're hoping to sell.

For Broken Armor I suggest:

Leather Leather Scraps
Composite Leather Scraps with Rivets or other hardware
Chain/Scale Small number of joined chain mail rings.
Plate Small metal stamping blanks

For armor you're hoping to sell, a small “Deed” can be used, it needs to incorporate the item # and description, but you can make as pretty as desired.

Crafting

For those of us who decide they want to participate in the crafting side of things, here's my thoughts on the rules that control crafting

On Field

The rules provided do a pretty good job of laying out all of the steps needed. For an Armorsmith, it's recommended to have a small anvil (real or foam) to act as your crafting prop.

When preparing to craft, make sure that you find a location you feel relatively safe at. A place where there are people to converse with is a plus, as the 2 hour requirement is a long time. I also suggest stocking up on drink and food, and using the restrooms.

When you're ready to start, get the attention of a PM to let them know that you'll be crafting.

After you're done crafting, let the GM shed know you've completed.
(I was told to tell econ, to get the temp tag, but Econ directed me to the GM Shed)

I recommend crafting the most expensive object you can (typically plate), as you're crafting only one item, regardless of the time it takes.

Between Events

Between events, you can craft either for yourself or for the King.

If you craft for the King, you will make silver based on your level in the craft.
Level 1 = Novice
Level 5 = Master.

If you craft for yourself, you can create any number of items that add up for the month. You'll need to make sure you have enough coin in the bank, or have the materials when you pick up the tag, or your crafting will fail.

You can craft and go on adventures at the same time.

In either route, you use the Item Creation link on the Kanar website. And fill it out. Next event, visit ECON to get your proper tag. Don't forget your physrep!!
(Above is the link as of the date of this last update, if it doesn't work for you please make sure to use the KANAR.CLUB website)

SubClasses

There are only a handful of subclasses that touch on armor specifically.

Champion

This subclass provides you with a free 10AP "helmet" every day. You can't wear a helmet to get the benefit, and it won't protect you against knockouts.

It will refresh to 10AP in the morning.

Since this is not a physical object, it doesn't interact with most of the armor rules.
So, I don't think it would be breakable, repairable, or destroyable.

Artisian

This subclass allows you to craft faster on field, giving you access to double the amount of time.
For armorsmithing, the extra time option isn't as vital, as the highest you could craft is plate, and that takes 2 weeks.
The extra item option is what we're interested in here.

I'm unclear on if the double crafting means you need to craft for 4 hours instead of 2.

Artificer

This is the upgrade of the Artisian subclass and adds quicker crafting on field.

I'm unclear on if this means that the double crafting takes place in 2 hours, or if crafting on field takes 1 hour.

Merging Locations

On facebook, the staff posted about the ability to combine multiple armor locations into a single tag.

This is only available for armor that is represented by a physrep that covers more than one location.
As a common example, a chain armor shirt could cover 3-5 locations and thus be merged into one tag.
The main benefit is that this merged tag has the total AP of all locations combined.

That would mean the the chain armor shirt mentioned above would have an AP of 45-75. So it would be broken after taking 45 damage, instead of each location breaking after 15 damage.


The drawback would be that the entire thing would have to be repaired together, which would take longer before you could use parts of it.

The rules around how enchanting is handled with the merging process still need to be worked out.
It's also unclear if you can merge different types of armor together, or if it has to be the same type.

To merge your locations, you'll need to talk to the Econ staff on field.

Appendix I

Appraisal

This skill essentially allows you to buy/sell armor at a more accurate amount.

Alchemy

This branch of skills costs more for each level you gain in it. As an example, Alchemy 1 costs 5 for a Mage, Alchemy 2 costs 10 for a Mage, etc…
It's essentially base cost x level. With a max of 9.

We're mostly just interested in level 4 here, as there is an alchemical oil called “Mending Oil” which will repair broken armor.
This is discussed in the Repair Options section .

Read/Write

This skill allows you to read and write a specific language.
It's the prerequisite of Alchemy and any of the elemental spell abilities.

Math

This skill allows for counting larger numbers and doing basic addition and subtraction.
Useful in general but is a Prerequisite of both Alchemy and More Math.

More Math

This skill allows you to do multiplication and division, which might be helpful in general.
We're interested in it because it's a Prerequisite of Alchemy level 4.

Spell Casting Abilities

These would be both the Magic Ability: Bardic Ability, E/W Ability, F/A Ability, and their Relevant spell slots.
Magic should probably have it's own guide, but for our purposes it's included for its ability to enhance or it's ability to provide MAP (Magical AP)
Please see the Magical Armor section for more information.

Metal Lore

This skill will give you knowledge of the different metals available and what their best propertiese are.
It will help during crafting, when picking out units for your armor so you know you're using the right metal.

It will also help if you go into enchanting, as you'll use it with Mystic Runes to make sure you have enchantable armor.

Appendix II — Special armors

Bronzewood and Chitinous Hide

This is unlocked for an Amorsmith at level 3
This armor is considered to be plate armor in all aspects but it's phsyrep, which is leather.
They can only be repaired by magical means.

If the armor is damaged but not broken, they must be fixed by a Mend spell.
If the armor is broken, it must be fixed by “Mending Oil”
